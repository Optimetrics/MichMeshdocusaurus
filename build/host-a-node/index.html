If you have a roof, you have a role

The network grows one rooftop at a time. Different properties serve the network differently — here's where you fit.

A home or small building

Even a regular rooftop can host a node that covers a neighborhood. If you have a clear sightline and a spot for a small solar panel, you're a candidate.

A barn, silo, or farm structure

These are gold. Tall, well-placed farm structures along rural corridors can extend the mesh for miles and fill gaps where cell service disappears.

A tower, water tank, or tall commercial building

The backbone of the network. A single well-placed tall-structure node can serve hundreds of families. We prioritize strategic commercial and municipal sites for free hardware and installation.

No structure, but you want to help

Join a group buy, host a meetup, or sponsor a node elsewhere. The community funds and builds nodes together — there's room for everyone.

What you're actually hosting

A MichMesh node is a small weatherproof box — about the size of a birdhouse — mounted somewhere with a clear view of the sky. Inside is a low-power radio and a small solar panel with a battery. It doesn't connect to your internet. It doesn't plug into your electrical. It doesn't have a camera or microphone. It can't see your network traffic or store any personal data.

All it does is listen for short text messages on a license-free radio frequency and pass them along to the next node. Think of it as a volunteer post office for radio signals — if a message comes in, it hands it off to whichever node is closest to the destination.

Most hosts forget the node is even there within a month. We check in once a year, replace a battery every five to seven years, and otherwise leave you alone.

For property owners

If you own a barn, silo, windmill, tower, or tall rooftop, your property might be exactly what the network needs. Strategic locations — especially along the lakeshore, through rural counties, and in cell dead zones — get priority for free hardware and installation.

What we provide

  • The node hardware, fully assembled
  • Solar panel and battery — no grid power needed
  • Weatherproof enclosure rated for Michigan winters
  • Professional installation at no cost for strategic sites
  • Annual check-ins and long-term maintenance

What we ask from you

  • Permission to mount the node on an existing structure
  • Roughly 30 minutes of your time during install
  • A way to reach you once a year for a wellness check

What hosting does not involve

  • No monthly bills, no subscriptions, no ongoing costs
  • No internet connection required
  • No power draw from your property
  • No access to your Wi-Fi, network, or devices
  • No cameras, microphones, or recording equipment

Parks departments, churches, townships, schools, and businesses make some of our best host partners. A node on your water tower, steeple, or rooftop serves hundreds of families in your service area at zero cost and zero liability.

Common questions

Is this legal?

Yes. MichMesh operates on FCC-approved license-free ISM band frequencies. No license, no permit, no paperwork required from you or us.

Can the node see my Wi-Fi or internet traffic?

No. The node has no connection to your network. It's a standalone radio that doesn't touch anything else on your property.

What if something goes wrong with it?

We handle it. If a node fails, we come out and fix or replace it at no cost to you.

Will it interfere with other electronics?

No. The transmit power is under 1 watt on frequencies shared safely with baby monitors, garage door openers, and cordless phones.

What does the node look like?

A small weatherproof box with a solar panel. Most hosts choose a discreet mounting location and the node blends into the existing structure.

Can I take it down if I change my mind?

Yes. Hosting is voluntary and there's no commitment. If you need it removed, we'll come get it — no hard feelings.

Who's liable if something happens?

MichMesh is a volunteer collective and does not carry liability insurance. That said, a node is passive hardware — no moving parts, no heat, no emissions — and is installed carefully on existing structures. For organizational hosts, we recommend a simple hosting agreement that spells out expectations on both sides; we provide a template.

Who actually runs MichMesh?

We're a volunteer collective of Michigan residents building this because it matters. No company, no paid staff, no ads. Just neighbors.
